Internet Adoption in Lebanon and the Middle East

Lebanon has achieved significant internet adoption, with roughly 90% of its population using the internet in 2023 [1]. Adoption across the Middle East varies: Gulf countries like the UAE and Qatar surpass 95%, while nations affected by conflict, such as Yemen and Syria, remain below 40% [2]. Mobile devices account for the majority of internet access, reflecting a global trend where mobile users outnumber desktop users [3].

Internet Infrastructure and Speeds

Lebanon has three major data centers and two active Internet Exchange Points (IXPs), supporting national digital infrastructure [1]. Average fixed broadband speeds are 7.5 Mbps, while mobile internet averages 15 Mbps. These metrics lag behind leading Gulf countries where fiber and 5G networks provide speeds above 100 Mbps [2]. Mobile traffic represents over 70% of internet usage, underscoring the need for mobile-first strategies [3].

Comparative Analysis and Trends

Internet penetration in Lebanon rose steadily from 82% in 2018 to 90% in 2023. Rural broadband access remains limited, highlighting regional disparities. Across the Middle East, mobile broadband subscriptions continue to expand, with Saudi Arabia and the UAE leading in 5G deployment [2]. Globally, internet users spend an average of seven hours online daily, primarily on social media, streaming, and e-commerce platforms [3].

Conclusion

Lebanon has made substantial progress in internet adoption and infrastructure, although broadband speeds lag behind regional leaders. Mobile access dominates usage, emphasizing the importance of mobile-first development for websites and applications. Continued investment in network infrastructure and equitable access will strengthen Lebanon’s growing digital ecosystem [1][2][3].
